  Lower Impact — Nice to Have

  - Unit conversion utilities — the library mixes SI throughout but users often work in imperial (lbf, inches, psia). A Units class would help at system boundaries.
  - CGNS binary export — currently just delegates to Plot3D. True CGNS would be compatible with more CFD solvers.
  - Dual-bell nozzle — altitude-compensating geometry that transitions between two contours; relevant for single-stage vehicles.
  - Coolant channel sizing — HeatTransferModel takes coolantHeatTransferCoeff as a given but doesn't compute it. A simple channel sizing sub-model (pressure drop, flow velocity, nucleate boiling margin) would close the loop.

  ---
  Lower temperatures
  The solver is also called at nozzle exit conditions (~1000–2000 K). At lower temperatures the equilibrium shifts heavily toward stable products (H2O, CO2), so these cases converge differently. Worth validating that the composition is correct there too.

  Lower pressures
  At lower chamber pressures (e.g., 0.5–2 MPa — typical of upper stages or thrusters) equilibrium shifts toward dissociation products. The pressure term in µⱼ matters more, and any pressure-dependent bug would show up here.

  Molecular weight and γ
  Rather than species fractions, compute the equilibrium mean molecular weight and specific heat ratio from the returned composition and NASA-7 polynomials, and compare against CEA's reported values. These are single numbers that are easy to verify and directly feed into c* and Isp, so errors in the
  chemistry propagate visibly.

  N₂O-containing propellants
  N₂O/ethanol or N₂O/propane tests would exercise the N-element path (N₂ species) which is currently untested. This matters because the N₂ species is in the solver but has no test coverage at all.

  Of these, I'd prioritise the O/F sweep first — it's the most likely to expose edge cases in the initial composition or damping logic — and molecular weight / γ validation second, since those connect directly to the performance calculations downstream.
